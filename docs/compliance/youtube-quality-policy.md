# YouTube Quality & Compliance Policy

## Reused content risk

Content that reuses existing material without meaningful transformation carries elevated policy and performance risk. All reused elements must add clear original value through commentary, analysis, structure, or educational framing.

## Repetitive content risk

Template-driven production can become repetitive when ideas, phrasing, and structure are recycled excessively. Teams must validate that each asset demonstrates topic-specific originality and editorial differentiation.

## Mass-produced content risk

High-volume generation pipelines increase the chance of thin, low-value output. QualityTube OS requires staged reviews and quality gates to prevent low-substance mass publishing behavior.

## Synthetic/altered content disclosure

Where synthetic or materially altered media is used, teams should disclose usage in context-appropriate ways consistent with platform and jurisdiction expectations.

## Misleading claims

Claims in scripts, titles, and descriptions must be supportable and non-deceptive. Unverifiable promises, fabricated evidence, and manipulative certainty statements are prohibited.

## Clickbait

Packaging should be compelling but accurate. Titles and thumbnails must not materially misrepresent the delivered content.

## Copyright risk

All assets must be rights-cleared or appropriately licensed. Teams are responsible for verifying source permissions for video, audio, images, and third-party text.

## Sensitive topics

Sensitive categories require additional review rigor for context, factual grounding, and harm minimization. Escalate uncertain cases for senior human review before publishing.

## Quality and originality requirements

Each published asset should demonstrate:
- clear user value
- coherent narrative structure
- factual and editorial consistency
- meaningful originality beyond prompt-level variation

## Human contribution requirement

A human contributor must materially shape the final output through review, revision, and explicit approval. Fully unattended model-to-publish workflows are out of policy.
